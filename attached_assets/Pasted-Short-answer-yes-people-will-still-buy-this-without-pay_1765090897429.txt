Short answer: yes, people will still buy this without payroll, and that’s actually an advantage for you right now.

* Tons of SaaS tools in HR don’t touch payroll: ATS (Greenhouse, Lever), assessment tools (Codility, HackerRank), onboarding tools, policy tools, etc. They all live **next to** payroll/HRIS and still print money.
* Payroll is a whole different beast: taxes, filings, benefits, jurisdiction rules. If you try to bolt that on now, you’re signing up for another massive regulatory headache.

**How to position it:**
You’re not “the system of record” (like ADP, Rippling). You’re the **hiring + decision support layer** that plugs into whatever payroll/HRIS they already use.

**Integration with payroll later:**
Pretty straightforward conceptually once you care about it:

* At MVP stage: export CSV/Excel of hired candidates with fields like name, email, title, salary, start date → they import into ADP/Gusto/Rippling/Paylocity.
* Later: add real integrations via APIs (most modern payroll/HRIS tools have REST APIs and/or webhooks).

  * Main work = auth (OAuth/API keys), field mapping, handling sync errors, and making sure your IDs line up (e.g., your “candidate_id” vs their “employee_id”).
* You don’t need any of that for v1. You just need clean exports and maybe a generic “webhook” integration.

So yes, absolutely viable without payroll. Don’t touch payroll until you have paying users begging for it.

4. Interview Assistant Module  
   - Given a job description, the system generates structured interview questions (behavioral and technical) and a simple scoring rubric.
