2. Build a real scoring framework (not vibes)
Right now your “Fit Score” & “Risk Score” are probably loose. If you want people to trust them, you need a clear formula, even if GPT helps.
Example:
Fit Score (0–100)
Let:
•	skills_match (0–100)
•	experience_years_match (0–100)
•	industry_match (0–100)
You decide weights:
Fit = 0.5 * skills_match + 0.3 * experience_years_match + 0.2 * industry_match
You can have GPT rate each dimension, but you control the combination.
Risk / Logic Score (0–100)
Break into factors:
•	Timeline consistency (no overlaps, believable durations)
•	Promotion realism
•	Job-hopping frequency
•	Vibe of accomplishments (specific vs vague)
•	AI/generic language score
Example:
Risk = 0.3 * timeline_risk +
       0.2 * promotion_risk +
       0.2 * job_hop_risk +
       0.15 * vagueness_risk +
       0.15 * ai_style_risk
You can compute some of these with hard rules:
•	Overlap > 6 months ⇒ timeline_risk 80+
•	5 jobs in 4 years ⇒ job_hop_risk 60+
•	Title jump from “Junior” to “Director” in < 2 years ⇒ promotion_risk 80+
Then let GPT refine/justify – but your rules make it consistent.

3. Timeline & promotions: make it visual and explicit
This is where your “Logic” angle can shine without being a CRA.
What to add:
•	Build a simple timeline visual:
o	Each job: bar from start to end.
o	Color overlap in red.
•	Label each transition:
o	Junior → Mid → Senior → Lead → Manager → Director → VP → C-level
•	Compute:
o	Average time per level
o	Total experience in target domain
o	Longest gap
In the UI, show:
•	“3 employers in last 2 years (potential job-hopping)”
•	“8-month unexplained gap between roles”
•	“Jump from Analyst → VP in 18 months (outlier; check manually)”
Then in the AI summary, explicitly point back to these facts.

4. Skills & JD matching: make it more specific
Right now you’re likely doing generic “matches many skills / missing a few.” That’s not enough.
Improvements:
1.	Extract and normalize skills into categories
o	Hard skills (Python, Excel, React)
o	Domain skills (logistics, payroll, compliance)
o	Soft skills (leadership, communication)
2.	From the job description, extract:
o	Must-have skills
o	Nice-to-have skills
o	Years required
o	Domain/industry
3.	In your UI:
o	Show 3 lists:
	✅ Matched must-have skills
	⚠️ Missing must-have skills
	➕ Bonus/extra skills
4.	Have GPT fill the lists but you keep the UI structure.
This makes the “Fit” feel real, not hand-wavy.
